## 大意

Anna 去 Tip Top Trading 面试 sales executive（销售主管）岗位。面试官是经理 Paul，秘书 Denise 不断送茶打断。Anna 在旁白提示下用了几个好表达推销自己，但最后一个问题还没回答——悬念留到 Episode 3。

## 原文（分场景）

### 场景 A — 公交车上（旁白 + Anna）

> This is Anna, on a bus, going to an interview for a job as a sales executive at Tip Top Trading, one of London's fastest growing companies. How are you feeling Anna?
>
> Oh, a little nervous, but I really want this job.
>
> Well, don't worry, Anna. As long as you say the right things, you'll be fine.
>
> The right things? Like what?
>
> You need to sell yourself. Be confident, not arrogant, and give examples.
>
> Like a good example that comes to mind.
>
> I'm particularly proud of...
>
> Timekeeping is important to me.
>
> Oh, right. Thanks. Perhaps you can come with me?
>
> Sorry, Anna. You're on your own now, but we'll be listening in.
>
> Look, you've just arrived. Good luck.

### 场景 B — 面试开始（Paul + Anna）

> Come in. Hello, I'm Paul, the manager of Tip Top Trading. And you must be...
>
> It's Anna. Yes, very good. Thanks for coming. Now, somewhere in this pile I got your CV.
>
> Er, is that it there?
>
> Oh yes, thanks, Hannah. Your qualifications look impressive, but I was wondering what sales experience can you bring to our company?
>
> I worked in a shop once.

### 场景 C — Anna 找到节奏（Anna + Paul + Denise 打断）

> Oh, right. Well, a good example that comes to mind is when I was involved with a campaign to promote and sell a new range of clothes. I loved doing it and it was...
>
> Oh, sorry. Excuse me. Here's your tea, Paul.
>
> Oh, thanks Denise. Now, Anna, it looks like you've achieved a lot during your time at university. Could you give me an example of good team working during your time there?
>
> Sorry, I forgot the sugar.
>
> Thank you, Denise.
>
> So you want an example? I was a treasurer of the debating society at university. That was OK, I suppose.
>
> Come on Anna! Be more enthusiastic! The debating society is exciting!
>
> I mean, I'm particularly proud of how I organised the finances for the debating society. We had a very small budget and I had to make decisions on what to buy.
>
> Very impressive. So you're a good planner, Anna. We like organised people here.

### 场景 D — 时间管理 + 最后问题

> Oops, silly me. I seem to have spilt tea over your CV.
>
> Oh, do you need some help?
>
> Oh no, I'm sure it'll dry out. Carry on, please.
>
> Also, timekeeping is important to me. I always try to complete my work on time. At university I never handed my assignments in late.
>
> That's good to hear. We like punctuality here.
>
> Excuse me Paul. Sorry it's a bit late but I thought you might like a biscuit with your tea.
>
> Hmm, thanks. Oh, lovely custard creams. Hmm.
>
> Now, Hannah, finally I wanted to ask you what exactly made you apply for this job at this company?
>
> Er, oh, well, the reason I applied is...
>
> Yes, yes, yes, Anna. Do you need a bit more time to think?
>
> Er, hmm...
>
> What's she going to say? How would you answer that? Join us next time to find out.

### 结尾 — 关键表达总结

> A good example that comes to mind.
>
> I'm particularly proud of...
>
> Timekeeping is important to me.

## 关键表达 Key Expressions

| 表达 | 用途 | 中文 |
|---|---|---|
| A good example that comes to mind is... | 举例子 | 我想到一个好例子…… |
| I'm particularly proud of... | 表达自豪（不吹嘘） | 我特别自豪的是…… |
| Timekeeping is important to me. | 强调守时 | 守时对我很重要。 |

## 连读精讲：失去爆破（Incomplete Plosion）

**规律**：两个爆破音（/p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, /g/）紧挨着时，前一个只做嘴型、不真正爆破，直接滑到下一个音。

**示例 — "Hold tight please!"**

| 写的 | 实际发音 | 原因 |
|---|---|---|
| hol**d** **t**ight | hol~~d~~-tight | /d/+/t/ 都是舌尖抵上颚，/d/ 不爆破 |
| tigh**t** **p**lease | tigh~~t~~-please | /t/+/p/ 相邻，/t/ 不爆破 |

听起来像 **"Hol-tai-please"**，三个词几乎粘成一团。

> 记忆钩子：爆破音连着爆破音 → 前面那个"只摆 pose 不出声"
